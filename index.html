Deliverables and Rationale

Here's a list of deliverables that we think will make this project a huge success:

  • Deliverable 1: A modern website that supports devices of all sizes.

  • Deliverable 2: Reorganize and prioritize content according to students' study flows.

  • Deliverable 3: Beautiful, clean and custom branding on top of a robust website framework.

  • Deliverable 4: Optimize content for in-class instructional settings.

  • Deliverable 5: Reimagine analog/paper content for the digital medium.

Deliverable 1: A modern website that supports devices of all sizes.

Rationale

Today's multi-device world requires that modern website support a myriad of devices with different screen sizes and form factors.

And with students being busier than ever, they should be able to study wherever and whenever they want, whether it be on their laptop at home or on their mobile device while commuting to school.

The new, modern website will feature:

  • Responsive layouts for mobile, laptop and desktop screen sizes.
  • Content optimized for smaller devices, including navigation and lecture content.

Deliverable 2: Reorganize and prioritize content according to students' study flows.

Rationale

We thought long and hard about how students will actually access and consume the content on the website.

After lots of brainstorming, we did a dramatic reimagining of how content is organized and delivered:

  • The primary organizing principle is around "content groupings" such as homework, lectures, labs, etc.
  • We've emphasized appropriate "chunking" of content, by enabling hiding/unhiding content.

We think that when students visit the website, they initially look for a particular type of content, whether it be lecture notes, homework, labs, or quizzes.

So, we've grouped content according to "content groups." These "content groups" will be among the first things the student sees when visiting the main website:

We've also emphasized the "chunking" of content, so that students can focus on a particular batch of content, one at a time. We think this will be a boon to students' ability to focus and get a deep understanding of the course material.

We achieved this through the use of accordians that will hide/unhide data as the student sees fit.

Deliverable 3: Beautiful, clean and custom branding on top of a robust website framework.

Rationale

We wanted the website to look and feel refreshingly modern, yet familiar.

Under the hood, we laid out the website on top of the world's most popular CSS framework, Bootstrap. We think this robust, open-source framework will serve as a strong foundation to our website.

On top of this, we've implemented custom styling and layouts that students will find familiar and aligned with the BCIT branding they're already used to.

The website will:

  • Utilize Bootstrap, the world's most popular CSS framework for building websites.
  • Have custom, fresh branding on top of a robust, open-source, and community-maintained framework.
  • Deliver a sense of familiar, yet refreshing brand identity

Deliverable 4: Optimize content for in-class instructional settings.

Rationale

So far, we've talked a lot about mobile/responsive design, but rest assured, we didn't forget about one of the most important places for learning: the classroom.

Today's classrooms utilize projectors as one of the most common delivery vehicles for content. So we made sure to optimize lecture content for projection for better readability and layout.

We also made sure to optimize content for printing as well.

To ensure a great experience for projection and printing:

  • You can toggle custom stylesheets for projectors on the fly
  • When projecting, we increase the font size and change the layout of some website components to put a spotlight on content first
  • When printing lecture content, only the essentials will show

Deliverable 5: Reimagine analog/paper content for the digital medium.

Rationale

Today, we're increasingly heading towards a "paperless" society. So we challenged ourselves to reimagine traditionally analog content to be served via a digital medium.

Would you like to see an example? One of the best examples of this is the transforming of a paper-administered quiz to an online quiz.

The online quiz will:

  • Be administered directly from the website.
  • Allow efficient delivery and collection of questions and answers
  • Be versatile for a myriad of different question/answer types, including paragraph, multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, etc.
